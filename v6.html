CVCooperator — V1

Optically-Controlled CV Shunt
Passive Core · Bus Powered Reference · Patch Driven Behavior

CVCooperator is a nonlinear optical CV processor. It mixes two modulation sources through light-dependent resistors, biases them against a controllable reference, and shapes the result with selectable lag.

It is not a precision tool. It is a controlled leak.

---

1. Channel Architecture (Locked)

---

2. Controls

  • MOD1 / MOD2 — Two CV modulation inputs
  • Level 1 / Level 2 — Per-source LED drive control
  • AMT — Global modulation strength (LED current scaling)
  • REF — Variable series resistance (compliance / ceiling)
  • LAG — 3-stage capacitor selection
  • OUT — Resulting CV output

AMT controls light intensity, not voltage directly. REF controls stiffness of the reference source.

---

3. Reference & Bus Decoupling

Reference voltage is derived from the Eurorack +5V rail. It is locally isolated and decoupled before distribution.

  • 1k–2.2k isolation resistor
  • 10µF electrolytic bulk capacitor
  • 100nF ceramic high-frequency capacitor

After decoupling, REF BUS behaves as a local stable source.

---

4. Electrical Characteristics

  • Unbuffered CV output
  • Designed for high-impedance Eurorack inputs
  • Nonlinear response due to LDR characteristics
  • Time response defined by R × C interaction

Not intended for pitch CV or audio-rate modulation.

---

5. Intended Use

  • VCF cutoff modulation
  • VCA level shaping
  • LFO rate warping
  • Envelope shaping
  • Slow chaotic modulation blending
---

6. Mechanical Format

  • Designed for 6HP single-channel format
  • Replicable per channel
  • Birch plywood panel compatible (engraved)
  • No internal multing
